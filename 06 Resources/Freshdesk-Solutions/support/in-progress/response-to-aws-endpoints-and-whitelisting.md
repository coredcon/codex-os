# Response to AWS endpoints and Whitelisting:

**QSR receives no advanced notice as to when the endpoints for AWS may change. We highly encourage the use of the wildcard in whitelisting. We could escalate your question regarding this specific endpoint, but it would take some time for this to be prioritized with the appropriate Development resource to research and confirm.  ****
****To answer your question as to why we include the specific endpoints at all; Customers that are unable to utilize wildcards for the whitelisting request a current list of all endpoints we are aware of at the time of their installation. QSR will always caution them to utilize wildcards, and then provide the most recent list we have on file.  I believe when they begin to see check-in failures, they use a network monitoring tool to identify any new endpoints and add them at that time.  QSR does not validate these updated / new endpoints each time they change.  ****
****If there is no way that (Company Name) Security team will authorize the wildcard method of whitelisting, the sites will likely continue to have check-in issues as the endpoints are updated by AWS.****
****If it would be beneficial, we could discuss scheduling a training session to review the license, registration and enterprise check in process in greater detail.  Let us know if this is something your team would be interested in.   Consistent connectivity to QSR's Enterprise service is crucial to your sites operations, and we will do our best to assist in arming (Company Name) with the information needed to affect this.**
