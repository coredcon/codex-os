# Support Guide to Chat

Starting December 9, the Support page on the QSR Automations website will have an option for customers to chat with the Support team. Selecting this option opens a chat window requesting customer details (name, company, email, phone number, and description of issue) and from which department they need assistance.  The chat will go into the Technical Support or Validation queue, depending on what the customer chooses.


**STEPS FOR WORKING A CHAT TICKET**


- [1. Accept the chat,]

- [2. Begin the conversation]

- [3. Create a ticket]

- [4. Work the ticket]

- [5. End the chat]

- [6. After closing the chat]


### **1. Accept the chat**


- Just like a phone call, you'll hear a ring and then see a notification on your Twilio Dashboard. Accept the chat by clicking the green checkmark.

- A chat window will open. You will see the customer details first, followed by an automated privacy notice.


### **2. Begin the conversation**


- There are canned responses for common situations. To access, open the canned response dropdown menu by clicking the blue + button and then the hamburger+ icon. You will be able to edit or delete the response before sending.

- You can use the _**Greeting** _canned response to provide your name and ask the customer what they need assistance with.

- Determine whether the customer has an account in Salesforce, if they are supported, or if they need to be referred to a reseller. You can use the _**Account Lookup**_ canned response if you require more information than has been provided.

- If they are not a current customer, determine which QSR team they will need to work with. 


### **3. Create a ticket**


- Open a new ticket in Freshdesk. You will choose Chat in the Requester Source dropdown menu, and use the customer-provided information to fill in the other fields. **Every chat must have an associated ticket.**


### **4. Work the ticket**


- **If inquiry needs to be handled by another QSR team:** 

- Use the _**Forward Information**_ canned response let them know you are forwarding their inquiry and contact information to the appropriate team (Client Services, Partner Assist, Sales, Orders, Partner Development, etc.), and someone should be reaching out to them soon. 

- Forward their information to the appropriate team in the ticket. 

- Note and close the ticket.


- **If the customer needs to be referred to their reseller:**

- Use the _**Send to Reseller**_ canned response to let them know their reseller is their first line of support

- Provide the name and contact number/email found on the reseller's account in Salesforce

- Note and close the ticket.


- **If site is unsupported:** 

- Use the _**Unsupported Version of CSK**_ canned response to let them know they are on an unsupported version, and you will forward their contact information to their Account/Channel Manager.

- Forward their information to Client Services/Partner Assist in the ticket. 

- Note and close the ticket.


- **For supported sites--work the ticket as usual:**

- If requesting logs, capture, etc., create a folder in Sharefile>Shared Folders>Support. The upload link can be sent through the chat. Make sure to include the link to the folder in your ticket notes.

- If a remote session is needed, a Zoho link can be sent through the chat.


- **If the customer needs to be transferred to the Validation team:**

- Use the _**Transfer to Validation**_ canned response to let the customer you are transferring them to the validation team to assist them with their license.

- Select the blue Transfer button at the top of the chat window.

- Select Queues and hover over Validations Queue to click on the arrows icon.


### **5. End the chat**


- If the issue can't be resolved during the chat:

- Use the _**Continue in Ticket**_ canned response to let them know that you will need to do further investigation and will be contacting them from the ticket.

- After the customer responds, use the appropriate _**Goodbye - UK/US** _canned response, provide the ticket number, and end the chat.


- Or if customer issue is resolved: 

- Use the _**Anything Else?**_ canned response to confirm the chat can be closed.

- Upon confirmation, or if the customer does not respond after 5 minutes, use the appropriate _**Goodbye - UK/US**_ canned response, provide the ticket number, and end the chat.


### **6. After closing the chat**


- Like a phone call, there is a brief WrapUp period before the task is marked Complete. You can select Complete to bypass this.

- After task has been marked Complete, use the clock icon on the left of the Twilio window to open Activity History.

- Hover over the left side of the chat conversation and select the eyeball icon to open Chat Details.

- Click the Add Note icon on the bottom left.

- Copy and paste the link to the associated ticket in the Notes field and click Save Note.

- Note the ticket and provide details of resolution in the summary once ticket is closed. If you need to view the chat transcript, hover over the left side of the chat conversation and select the hamburger icon to View Chat History.
